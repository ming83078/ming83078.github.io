Digital End-to-End Processes

Story Background

The DEEP project aims to analyze, optimize and digitalize SHL Medical's operational value creating processes end-to-end, supported by an improved IT deployment including several systems implemented in the operation and product streams

This is the story of how I planned and engaged in UX research in the MES (Manufacturing Execution System) stream of the Operation Stream to define detailed requirements and concepts including hardware and software for the future MES system for medical.

(Note: According to confidentiality policy, the details of the project can not be presented. Therefore, the portfolio focus on showing the know-how of the UX work process from my own experience.)


My Role

I planned and conducted context inquiries, expert interviews, and usability tests on the shop floor of the business departments to define user requirements and evaluate prototypes. In addition, I cooperated with external business consultants to conducted physical and virtual design thinking workshops to define high-level needs with stakeholders. UX is not only a methodology to define user requirements and design usable applications but also a strategy to facilitate change management of work process digitalization in this project.


Process of defining requirements and designing prototypes

Different UX methods were used in the project phases of feasibility and concept to define end users' and stakeholders' needs.







1. Context inquiries, work instructions and production records analysis

Desk research was first conducted to understand high-level workflows and how the production data were manually recorded. After that, context inquires were conducted on the shop floor of the business departments including injection molding, assembly, shield remover, tray production, spring production, and printing. The AEIOU (activity, environment, interaction, object, user) method was used to collect and analyze data from field observations and interviews.




2. Physical and virtual design thinking workshops

To further understand the workflows on the shop floor of each business department and define user requirements, key stakeholders (i.e., managers, team leaders, line supervisors, quality assurance audits) were invited to design thinking workshops. Due to the pandemic situation, virtual workshops were also conducted. The outputs of the workshops were low-fidelity prototypes created among the attendees that integrated and represented stakeholdersâ€™ needs.




3. Expert interviews

High-fidelity prototypes were designed and created by the project team. I used these prototypes to conducted expert interviews to improve the design. Supervisors who were familiar with front-line work and experienced end-users such as operators and technicians were invited to the interviews.




4. Usability tests

After the prototype iterations according to the results of expert interviews, I conducted a usability test with end-users in a simulated shop floor environment. The tests aim to have their feedback for improving the design that supports them to digitalize production records. The users included Taiwanese and Filipino operators and technicians. The results were the inputs of the User Requirement Specifications and the User Interface Design Guideline.




Impacts

The outputs were used to the selected to-be concept and evaluate hardware and software supplier solutions.



The methods and process of these UX research activities provided a channel for business departments to specifically describe their down-to-earth needs and empowered end-users to show their opinions. It can be seen as a strategy to increase the acceptance of new workflows and applications during the change management process.
