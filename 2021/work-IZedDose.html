Story Background

The InsulCheck Dose project is a customer project from Innovation Zed in Ireland. The Device is a class I medical device that aims to aid people with diabetes to manage their injection times and dosages well. As a result, they can increase their medication adherence and facilitate their life qualities.

This is the story of how I implemented the usability engineering (UE) process in the Proof of Concept (POC) and Design Phase of the project and then drove the design from the UE outcomes.

(Note: According to confidentiality policy, the details of the project can not be presented. Therefore, the portfolio focus on showing the know-how of UX work process from my own experience.)



My Role

As the UX engineer in the project team, I am responsible for ensuring the design is MDR ( Medical Device Regulation) compliant from the usability perspective. I have created UE file with use specification, task analysis, use-related risk assessment, user interface specifications and conducted user studies and formative evaluations to improve safe and efficient usage of the device.


The Challenges

The challenges were not only from custormer's and the end users' needs but also from the Quality Management System wise of the Engineering Solutions Deparment that was responsible to provide the design service.



Challenge 1: How to practice the first in-house usability engineering process in the department?

More than six months ahead of the project kick-off, the Usability Engineering Guide was created by myself and approved by the department QA and PM leads.

In the early stage of the project POC phase, the usability activities and deliverables of which complied with the guide were conducted to prepare the preliminary outcomes.

During the middle stage of the project POC phase, a hatching-hour speech was given by myself to communicate the value and importance of implementing the UE Guide. The communication was efficient because the theoretical (international regulations and the effective UE Guide) and practical evidence (UE deliverables) were well prepared and the implementation provided an understandable example.



Challenge 2: What are the use behaviours of insulin injectors?

Two online surveys, six in-depth interviews and desk research were conducted. Totally, 87 participants with diabetes from Ireland, Finland, U.K., and Taiwan participated in the activities and provided valid data. The results provided the inputs to the use scenarios and user requirements of the Device.



Challenge 3: User-centered Design: How does the UE outcome drives the design?

The structural usability deliverables provide solid evidence to define the user interface specifications that iterate the System Requirement Specification. In addition, a clear and patient communication approach is vital to facilitate the impacts of usability engineering in the project.



Impacts

On the project and design

In the Proof of Concept Phase of the project, more than 75% use errors were mitigated according the results of formative evaluation. The verified user interface specifications were transferred to the System Requirement Specifications. For instance:

  • The latch is easy to open and does not hurt users’ nails.
  • The dimension of the Device does not interfere with performing hypodermic injections. Most users did not cover the whole screen while hypodermic injections and felt that covering the screen while hypodermic injections did not matter.



On the department

After the implementation of UE process in the project, the outcomes are admitted by the project team and more projects that start from the feasibility phase.

  • “Usability can change a lot.”– the project PM
  • Members consider that how will the users feel about the design and ask the usability engineer for inputs and conducting related user studies more often



Know More about the Device

The InsulCheck Dose is the new version of a family of products, from simple timer devices, to Bluetooth® Low Energy (BLE) enabled products, capable of tracking pen usage, injection events, temperature and dialed dosage.
